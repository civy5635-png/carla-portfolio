My Journey Into Web Design and Digital Business

After 30 years of working retail and living paycheck to paycheck, I retired due to disability in 2023 and realized I needed to completely reinvent myself. Faced with an uncertain future, I chose to embrace change rather than be defined by limitations, committing myself to learning new skills that could lead to long-term independence.

With little computer experience beyond online shopping and taxes, I began teaching myself web design, digital marketing, automation software, SEO, and online business tools from scratch to build a sustainable future working from home. What began as a necessity quickly became a genuine passion for design, problem-solving, and helping businesses improve their digital presence.

As my skills grew, so did my vision. This journey ultimately led to the creation of Ella Digital Consulting, a business focused on helping small businesses strengthen their online reputation, improve visibility, and leverage automation software to save time and accelerate growth

My story is proof that reinvention is possible at any stage of life, and that persistence, adaptability, and curiosity can create entirely new opportunities.

In Defense of AI

In the process of learning, I discovered ChatGPT. Although I can’t deny the concerns surrounding AI — including environmental impact, ethical questions, and the way creative work is used to train AI systems — I also can’t ignore the positive impact it has had on my life.

For me, AI became a learning partner. It gave me a place to ask basic questions without embarrassment, break down technical problems into language I could understand, and troubleshoot issues one step at a time. Over the last two years, it has helped me learn skills I once thought were completely out of reach.

I don’t see AI as a replacement for effort, judgment, or creativity. I see it as a tool — one that still requires curiosity, critical thinking, and persistence from the person using it. Learning how to use it responsibly has helped me keep going when the learning curve felt overwhelming.

Every new skill I learn brings me closer to my goal of building websites and automation tools for small businesses. Those black screens with code no longer scare me quite so much — which is a sentence I never expected to write.
